How to play
Goal: clear every ★ required directive before its deadline. Optional directives and a high prestige score earn a MAJOR win — but you can't do everything.

Place buildings (free) from the palette: tap one, then tap a highlighted tile. Earth only delivers a few per tier each turn (the T1/T2/T3 counts). Nothing is stockpiled — every turn each building turns input flows into output flows; spare output feeds your active directives.

Population is your workforce. Habitats provide housing capacity; colonists arrive by immigration (a fixed number per turn — directives raise it) and fill empty housing over time. You can't conjure workers on demand — grow housing ahead of need. Life support (food/water/power per colonist) is paid before any industry; if it falls short, immigration pauses until you fix it.

Directives are sustained goals: "supply ≥ X /turn for D turns". Each turn you clear the bar counts once; meeting it consumes that surplus, so directives compete for the same goods. Completing one only unlocks its dependents next turn.

Placement matters. Extractors need their deposit. Solar is stronger toward the sunward (left) edge. A building gets +22% per adjacent tile producing one of its inputs — cluster your chains. Heat plants (♨) want an adjacent Radiator or they throttle. Reactors irradiate neighbours (☢), cutting Habitat housing and Greenhouse output — unless on a lava tube (which boosts Habitat capacity ×1.4). When placing, each legal hex shows the multiplier you'd get there.

Tap any tile to see what it's doing, what's throttling it, and every active bonus. Undo rewinds your moves — including past an End Turn.
